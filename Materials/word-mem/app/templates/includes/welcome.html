About the study

Thanks for taking part! The study was about how we remember words or images that have different amounts of information. Information in this context means something quite specific, but roughly, it relates to common something is: rare words like ocelot are generally more informative than more common words like cat. The list of words and images we gave you had mixed up rare and common words and items in a particular way, either by mixing them throughout the list or clumping them all at the begining or end. We're looking to see how the rarity of words or images, and the order in which they're presented can help (or hinder!) how well people can process and recall them.
